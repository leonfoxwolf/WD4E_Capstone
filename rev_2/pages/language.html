Japanese Language

I bet that many of those reading this text think that Japanese is something very complex and transcendental. And they think wrong, I tell you. Japanese is the same as other languages. Personally, my opinion: what to learn, say, English, and what Japanese - there is basically no difference: both have their own peculiarities.

Like all languages, Japanese is divided into spoken and written. By the way, if someone wanted to study this, I highly recommend the following book: "First Japanese Lessons" (by Naoe Naganuma), a reddish hardcover book (sold in "Biblio Globus"). This book is very simple and accessible for studying the oral part of such an "inaccessible" language.
